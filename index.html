Design can change the world

Design is more than just a visual veneer. Design shapes science, technology, business and culture into human experiences. Design is the application of empathy, creativity, and understanding to solve genuine human needs.

Great design can make life better, meaning clearer, and action effortless. When applied with technology, design can transform daily activities or entire industries.

Change is constant

Digital products are moving away from fixed visual interfaces and into flexible, changing ecosystems of services - physical, digital, social, conversational, predictive - that each person will experience slightly differently.

In this environment of continuous development, design is the unifying voice that creates a sense of coherence and continuity.

This means that design must adapt and grow too. Design isn’t a compartmentalised process, it’s a living part of a business.

Innovation is not a luxury

Innovative products and communications aren’t a luxury, they are crucial to standing out in the crowded digital landscape.

Customers are never more than a click away from competitors. Design means constantly evaluating, iterating and evolving products to keep competitors at bay, grow sales, and keep customers engaged.

Technology is for humans

Laurie Anderson said “technology is the camp-fire around which we tell our stories.” Telling stories is a great way to keep technological conversations focused on people.

Building products using stories as a guide helps the people that are making them. Making products that have a story to tell helps customers connect with them.

Designers must work backwards from human needs to craft into our products the stories that resonate with our customers.

Moments matter

Looking at the big picture of how experiences fit together gives us a better view of what individual steps need to deliver. We need to look at the whole customer journey.

If we consider how previous steps will effect customer’s needs and feelings, and what future steps we need to prepare for: we can make the current moment more effective.

That big picture also gives us a view on which steps in the journey will be the most effective, or offer the greatest opportunity. This helps us to focus on developing experiences that have the greatest potential for impact.

Create moments that

Deliver Value

Pivotal customer steps that could impact conversions, revenue, volume or extend lifetime value.

Create moments that

Are Human

Opportunities to show empathy with customers to deepen trust, build relationships and nurture them to build long-term value.

Create moments that

Reduce Costs

Experiences that can reduce complexity, streamline expensive back-office processes or cut demand on customer support.

Create moments that

Stand Out

Moments that are memorable, that capture brand values and make products desirable and delightful.

Design is based on evidence

Innovation is rooted in insight. Revelations about the marketplace, technology trends and customer’s needs allows you to create better product/market fit.

Engaging with real customers at all levels reveals emergent patterns, challenges, painpoints, and opportunities that can be addressed by innovation.

Research, combined with creativity and intuition, identifies new opportunities and creates pathways to deliver them.

Status Quo Research

Reveals the competitive marketplace a product needs to compete in, establishing norms, expectations and criteria needed to stand out.

Generative Research

Is participatory, engaging stakeholders and real customers. It also brings a wider view - such as looking for analogies and parallels in other sectors.

Formative Research

Establishes what needs to be built and how it will succeed using tangible prototypes to probe and refine idaes. Emphasising experimentation, reinterpretation and learning.

Evaluative Research

Increases confidence in which routes to take to market by identifying approaches, features and touch-points that customers most value.
