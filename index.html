Design can change the world

Design is more than just a visual veneer. Design shapes science, technology, business and culture into human experiences. Design is the application of empathy, creativity, and understanding to solve genuine human needs.

Great design can make life better, meaning clearer, and action effortless. When applied with technology, design can transform daily activities or entire industries.

Designers must give the things we make a purpose. With purpose comes meaning, resonance, and a way to gauge impact.

Change is constant

Digital products are moving away from fixed visual interfaces and into flexible, changing ecosystems of services - physical, digital, social, conversational, predictive - that each person will experience slightly differently.

In this environment of continuous development, design is the unifying voice that creates a sense of coherence and continuity.

This means that design must adapt and grow too. Design isn’t a compartmentalised process, it’s a living part of a business.

Technology is for humans

Laurie Anderson said “technology is the camp-fire around which we tell our stories.” Telling stories is a great way to keep technological conversations focused on people.

Building products using stories as a guide helps the people that are making them. Making products that have a story to tell helps customers connect with them.

Designers must work backwards from human needs to craft into our products the stories that resonate with our customers.

Innovation is not a luxury

Innovative products and communications aren’t a luxury, they are crucial to standing out in the crowded digital landscape.

Customers are never more than a click away from competitors. Design means constantly evaluating, iterating and evolving products to keep competitors at bay, grow sales, and keep customers engaged.
